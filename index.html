About Jose Rizal

The National Hero of the Philippines and pride of the Malayan Race. He was born on June 19, 1861, in the town of Calamba, Laguna.

He was the seventh child in a family of 11 children (2 boys, 9 girls) of Francisco Mercado Rizal and Teodora Alonzo y Quintos.

Baptized by Rev. Ruffino Collantes at the Catholic Church of Calamba with Rev. Pedro Casanas as sponsor on June 22, 1861.

Rizal's Education

Early Education

Rizal began his education at home under the guidance of his mother, Teodora Alonso. At the age of three, he began to learn the alphabet, and by age five, he could already read and write.

Formal Education in Binan, Laguna:

At the age of 9, Rizal was sent to Biñan to study under Justiniano Aquino Cruz, a teacher who taught him Latin and other subjects.

Ateneo Municipal de Manila

In 1872, at the age of 11, Rizal enrolled in the Ateneo Municipal de Manila, a prominent Jesuit school. There, he studied various subjects including Latin, Spanish, Greek, French, English, and history.

University of Santo Tomas

Rizal pursued higher education at the University of Santo Tomas, where he enrolled in the Bachelor of Arts program. However, he left the university due to discrimination against Filipino students by Spanish faculty members.

Universidad Central
de Madrid

Rizal traveled to Spain in 1882 to pursue higher education. He enrolled at the Universidad Central de Madrid (now known as Complutense University of Madrid), where he pursued a degree in Medicine and later, a degree in Philosophy and Letters.

University of Paris (Sorbonne)

Rizal continued his studies in Europe and enrolled at the University of Paris (Sorbonne), where he pursued a second doctorate in Ophthalmology.

Heidelberg University

Rizal also studied at Heidelberg University in Germany, where he continued his training in ophthalmology.

Travels

  • 1882-1887

    Europe

    Rizal's first major travel was to Europe to pursue higher education. He lived in various European cities during this time, including Madrid, Paris, Heidelberg, and Wilhelmsfeld. Rizal studied at several prestigious universities, including the Universidad Central de Madrid, University of Paris (Sorbonne), and Heidelberg University.

    Philippines


    Rizal returned briefly to the Philippines in 1887 after completing his studies in Europe. However, his stay was short due to political tensions with Spanish authorities.

    1887

  • 1888

    Japan


    While returning to Europe from the Philippines, Rizal made a brief stopover in Japan. He spent a short time in Yokohama and Tokyo, observing Japanese culture and society.

  • 1888

    Hong Kong


    Rizal visited Hong Kong during his journey back to Europe. He stayed for a short period before continuing his travels.

    1888

  • 1890

    Spain


    Rizal returned to Spain in 1890 to advocate for reforms in the Philippines. He became actively involved in the Propaganda Movement, writing articles for La Solidaridad, a newspaper published in Barcelona.

  • 1892

    Philippines


    Rizal returned to the Philippines in 1892 after his exile in Dapitan. He established the La Liga Filipina, a civic organization aimed at promoting reforms and Filipino representation in the Spanish government.

    1892

  • 1892

    Hong Kong


    After the suppression of the La Liga Filipina, Rizal fled to Hong Kong to avoid arrest by Spanish authorities. He continued his advocacy work from exile and wrote his second novel, "El Filibusterismo," during this time.

  • 1892

    China


    While in Hong Kong, Rizal also made a brief visit to southern China, including Macau and Canton.

    1892

  • 1892

    Singapore


    Rizal made a stopover in Singapore during his travels in Southeast Asia. He spent some time in the city, meeting with fellow Filipino expatriates.

  • 1896

    Spain


    Rizal briefly returned to Spain in 1896 before traveling to Cuba to serve as a military doctor during the Spanish-American War.

    1896

  • 1892

    Cuba


    Rizal traveled to Cuba to offer his medical services during the Spanish-American War, intending to support the Cuban revolutionaries.

  • 1896

    Philippines


    Rizal returned to the Philippines for the last time in 1896, intending to participate in the Philippine Revolution. However, he was arrested upon his arrival in Manila and later executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896, at Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park) in Manila.
